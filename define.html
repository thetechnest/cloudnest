What is the Define Stage?

The Define stage focuses on clearly articulating the core problem based on insights gathered during the Empathize stage. Instead of listing symptoms, this stage identifies the root cause that must be addressed through design.

üéØ Define converts observations into a clear problem statement.

Inputs from the Empathize Stage

From empathy interviews and observations with NBKRIST students, the following key insights were identified:

  • Students feel anxious during result declaration
  • Marks alone do not explain performance
  • Students cannot identify strengths and weaknesses
  • There is no guidance for improvement after results
  • Faculty explanations are required repeatedly

Problem Statement

NBKRIST students need a transparent, analytical, and student-centric way to understand academic results, because traditional result systems only display marks without providing insights, trends, or improvement guidance.

How Might We (HMW) Questions

Based on the problem definition, ‚ÄúHow Might We‚Äù questions were framed to guide solution ideation without limiting creativity.

  • How might we help students understand their performance beyond marks?
  • How might we reduce result-related stress using insights?
  • How might we visualize strengths and weaknesses clearly?
  • How might we support students in planning academic improvement?

Why the Define Stage is Critical for Software Design

In software development, poorly defined problems often lead to feature overload or unusable systems. The Define stage ensures that technology solves the right problem before writing any code.

  • Prevents unnecessary or confusing features
  • Aligns system goals with user needs
  • Improves clarity for developers and stakeholders
  • Acts as a foundation for ideation and prototyping

Outcome of the Define Stage

The Define stage produced a clear and focused problem statement that directly guided the ideation of ResultPulse features such as dashboards, analytics, and performance insights.
