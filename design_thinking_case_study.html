Improving College Canteen Food Ordering Experience

Introduction

Design Thinking is a human-centered approach that focuses on understanding user needs and developing innovative solutions. In a college environment, students often face issues such as long queues, order confusion, and delays in the canteen. This mini project applies Design Thinking principles to redesign the food ordering experience.

Problem Statement

Students experience long waiting times, incorrect orders, and inconvenience while ordering food in the college canteen, especially during peak hours.

Objectives

  • Reduce waiting time
  • Improve order accuracy
  • Enhance student satisfaction

Proposed Solution

A QR-code based digital ordering system that allows students to place orders using mobile phones and collect food without standing in long queues.

Expected Benefits

  • Reduced crowding
  • Faster service
  • Improved accuracy
  • Better user experience

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Design Thinking enables innovative, user-centered solutions. By focusing on empathy and iterative improvement, the canteen experience can be significantly enhanced.
