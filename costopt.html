Cost Optimization in Cloud Computing

What is Cost Optimization?

Cost optimization in cloud computing is the practice of reducing unnecessary cloud spend while maintaining performance, reliability, and scalability.

Unlike traditional data centers, cloud costs are dynamic. Poor design decisions can silently increase monthly bills.

üí° Key Idea: Cost optimization is a continuous process, not a one-time activity.

Why Cost Optimization is Critical

  • Pay-as-you-go pricing can lead to unexpected bills
  • Over-provisioning wastes resources
  • Idle services still generate costs
  • AI/ML workloads can scale costs rapidly

Core Cost Optimization Strategies

1. Right Sizing ‚Äì Match resources to actual workload needs.

2. Elastic Scaling ‚Äì Scale up during peak, scale down during low usage.

3. Resource Scheduling ‚Äì Shut down unused resources.

4. Storage Tiering ‚Äì Use cheaper storage for infrequently accessed data.

5. Monitoring & Alerts ‚Äì Track usage and spending in real time.

Cost Optimization in the AI/ML Era

AI/ML workloads amplify cloud costs due to compute-intensive training, frequent retraining, and large datasets.

  • Use spot/preemptible instances for training
  • Separate training and inference workloads
  • Monitor model usage and performance drift
