What is Empathize?

Empathize is the first and most critical stage of Design Thinking. It focuses on understanding users deeply by observing, interacting, and listening to them. Instead of assuming problems, designers try to experience the users‚Äô emotions, challenges, and expectations.

üéØ In Design Thinking, empathy means designing with users, not for users.

Why Empathy Matters to a Software Designer

Software designers often focus on features and technology. However, without empathy, even technically perfect software can fail to satisfy users.

  • Helps designers understand real user pain points
  • Prevents building unnecessary or confusing features
  • Improves usability, trust, and user satisfaction
  • Aligns software behavior with human emotions

Understanding Students of NBKRIST (Project Context)

In the ResultPulse project, empathy was focused on understanding the academic and emotional challenges faced by NBKRIST students during result declaration.

  • Fear and anxiety while checking results
  • Confusion due to marks-only presentation
  • Inability to identify strengths and weaknesses
  • Pressure from parents and peers
  • Lack of guidance after result publication

How Empathy Was Conducted in This Project

The empathy stage was conducted using multiple qualitative methods to capture real student experiences.

  • Informal discussions with students after result declaration
  • Observation of student reactions during result days
  • Interaction with faculty to understand evaluation concerns
  • Listening to student questions and repeated doubts

Empathy Interview ‚Äì Scripted Interaction

To understand students‚Äô real experiences beyond assumptions, a scripted empathy interview was conducted. The goal was not to judge performance, but to listen carefully and capture emotional and cognitive challenges faced during result declaration.

Outcome of the Empathize Stage

The empathy stage revealed that the core issue was not poor performance, but poor interpretation of performance. Students needed clarity, reassurance, and actionable insights rather than just marks.
