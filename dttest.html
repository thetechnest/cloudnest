What is the Test Stage?

The Test stage evaluates the prototype by involving real users to understand how well the solution addresses their needs. It is not limited to functional correctness, but focuses on usability, clarity, and user experience.

User Involvement in the Test Stage

Testing was conducted by actively involving NBKRIST students and faculty members to ensure realistic evaluation.

  • Students used the prototype during simulated result scenarios
  • Faculty reviewed analytics and interpretation accuracy
  • Users were encouraged to speak freely about their experience
  • Observations were recorded without interference

Feedback Loop & Iteration

Feedback obtained during testing was analyzed and used to refine the prototype iteratively. This feedback loop ensures continuous improvement of the solution.

  • Simplified dashboard labels based on student feedback
  • Improved graph readability and color usage
  • Added explanatory tooltips for analytics
  • Refined navigation flow for clarity

Why the Test Stage is Critical

Without testing, design decisions remain assumptions. The Test stage validates ideas with real users and ensures that the solution delivers actual value.

  • Reduces risk of user rejection
  • Improves user satisfaction
  • Ensures alignment with real needs
  • Completes the Design Thinking lifecycle
