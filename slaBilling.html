A Service Level Agreement (SLA) is a formal commitment by a cloud provider that defines expected service availability, performance, and reliability.

Simple Meaning: SLA is a promise about uptime and service quality.
  • Defines uptime percentage (e.g., 99.9%)
  • Specifies responsibilities
  • Includes service credits for failures
üìò Exam Line: SLA specifies guaranteed service availability and compensation.

Why SLA is Important for Organizations

  • Ensures business continuity
  • Builds customer trust
  • Supports mission-critical workloads
  • Reduces operational risk
No SLA ‚Üí No confidence in running production systems.

Cloud billing is based on usage. Organizations pay only for the resources they consume instead of investing in fixed infrastructure.

Traditional IT: Capital Expenditure (CapEx)
Cloud: Operational Expenditure (OpEx)
  • Compute billed per second or minute
  • Storage billed per GB per month
  • Network billed per data transfer
üìò Exam Line: Cloud billing follows a pay-as-you-go pricing model.

Billing and Cost Management Services

AWS: Cost Explorer, Budgets, Pricing Calculator
GCP: Billing Reports, Cost Table
Azure: Cost Management + Billing

These tools help organizations track usage, forecast costs, and avoid unexpected bills.

Why Billing is a Strategic Keyword

  • Controls operational expenses
  • Enables cost optimization
  • Supports scalable growth
  • Aligns IT spending with business value

Final Summary

Service Level Agreements ensure trust and reliability, while cloud billing ensures flexibility and cost efficiency. Together, they form the foundation of sustainable cloud adoption for modern organizations and AI-driven systems.
